Crying Tiger Steak

Recipe from Ian Kewks

Thai Crying Tiger Steak is a mouthwatering dish that is sure to please any steak lover. With its umami-bomb marinade and spicy dipping sauce, this beef dish is perfect for any occasion!

Ingredients

  • 600-800 g steak
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil

Steak Marinade

  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp neutral oil
  • 0.5 tbsp palm sugar
  • .25 tsp ground black pepper

Nam Jim Jeaw

  • 1.5 tsp uncooked rice grains, toasted and ground
  • 2 tbsp lime juice
  • 1 tbsp fish sauce
  • 1 tbsp tamarind paste
  • 1 tbsp palm sugar
  • 1 tbsp cilantro, chopped
  • 1-2 tsp chili flakes
  • 0.5 shallot, cut into wedges

Directions

  1. Mix together the soy sauce, oyster sauce, oil, palm sugar, and ground black pepper in a bowl until combined. Let the steaks marinate for at least one hour to overnight in the fridge.
  2. Once marinated, remove the steak from the fridge and let come to room temperature.
  3. Heat a heavy bottom pan or iron skillet over medium-high heat with 2 tbsp of neutral oil. Fry the marinated steaks until you reach your desired doneness, flipping frequently to avoid overly burning the sugars.
  4. Set aside, and let rest for at least 10 minutes while you prepare the dipping sauce.
  5. Heat a dry pan over medium heat. Once hot, add the glutinous rice to the pan and toss/stir continuously until the grains have become golden brown in color.
  6. Transfer the toasted grains to a mortar and pestle and grind the rice until you achieve a coarse powder.
  7. Add in the lime juice, fish sauce, tamarind paste, palm sugar, chopped cilantro, Thai chili flakes, and shallots. Mix until well combined and adjust accordingly to tastes.
  8. Once the steak has adequately rested, slice into strips. Serve the steak alongside the nam jim jeaw and fresh vegetables. Enjoy!
